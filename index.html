Our Platform

The United Students of Miguel strives to increase liberty in all of Mr. Miguel’s classes.

The lack of seating freedom within classes oppresses students and leads to a feeling of isolation from their companions. Although teachers want to increase productivity in their classrooms, seating charts come with the price of discouraging communication and self-advocacy. Open classroom seating has a profound impact on student motivation and achievement. Assigned classroom seating is mainly a question of whether or not teachers want their students to succeed to their highest potential. To an extent, control makes education less desirable.

For years, teachers have used seating charts with good intentions, believing that students would make new friends and learn to talk to others with confidence. However, the underlying issue of the struggle for control is not solved by the implementation of assigned seating. Trust is the foundation for any successful teacher-student relationship. The amount of trust between students and their teachers plummets when seating charts are created and imposed upon students.

Implementing unassigned and flexible seating is a shift in both structure and teaching philosophy. Teachers must be willing to give up the power of the seating chart and fully trust their students to select their seats wisely and responsibly. Students will become more aware of what types of seating and learning environment suits them best, empowering them to take more opportunities to learn.

Why You Should Care

Open seating will allow each and every student to learn in an environment that is supportive and comfortable. Students all learn in different ways and at different paces, meaning that seating charts force every student to learn the same as their tablemates, even if they have conflicting ways and speeds of learning. Students should be comfortable and feel at ease when in class, all the while feeling that they’re learning to the best of their ability, at their own speed, and not as if they’re either being rushed or dragged.
